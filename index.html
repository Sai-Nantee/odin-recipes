2. Chicken Fried Chicken


Chicken Fried:  A fun chicken recipe the kids can help prepare. They love crushing the crackers. It does not matter if the measurements aren't perfect, just wing it!

Ingredients

  • 30 saltine crackers
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons dry potato flakes
  • 1 teaspoon seasoned salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 6 skinless, bonelss chicken breast halves
  • 2 cups vegetable oil for flying

Steps

  1. Step 1: Place crackers in a large resealable plastic bag; seal bag and crush crackers with a rolling pin until they are coarse crumbs. Add the flour, potato flakes, seasoned salt, and pepper and mix well.
  2. Step 2: Beat egg in a shallow dish or bowl. One by one, dredge chicken pieces in egg, then place in bag with crumb mixture. Seal bag and shake to coat.
  3. Step 3: Heat oil in deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  4. Step 4: Fry chicken, turning frequently, until golden brown and juices run clear, 15 to 20 minutes.

3. Miso Soup


This miso soup is full of savory flavors. Add more dashi to your soup if you want a stronger stock. You can use yellow, white, or red miso paste for this soup — yellow miso is sweet and creamy, red miso is stronger and saltier.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons dashi granules
  • 3 tablespoons miso paste
  • 1 (8 ounce) package sillken tofu, diced
  • 2 green onions, sliced diagonally into 1/2 inch pieces

Steps

  1. Step 1: Gather all ingredients.
  2. Step 2: Combine water and dashi granules in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat; bring to a boil.
  3. Step 3: Reduce heat to medium and whisk in miso paste.
  4. Step 4: Stir in tofu.
  5. Step 5: Separate the layers of green onions, and add them to the soup. Simmer gently for 2 to 3 minutes before serving.
